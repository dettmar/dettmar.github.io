For me, a creative solution must solve a problem better than before, in a new, smarter, cheaper, faster way

Preparation

  • Create an "oasis" of space and time
    • Define a clear start and end time (usually 1.5h)
    • No other todo's for now
    • Trivial things that are urgent are easier to do than important things that are not urgent
  • Create an environment for trust and enjoyment for all
  • Allow each other to be stupid/illogical/funny

Do not stress the solution

Tolerate the discomfort of not having one

Too many bad projects go this path

Existing work culture

(How do I sell this to my boss?)

  • Chances are, your company does not actively reflect upon how things are done
  • Monkeys in a cage
  • Having fun doesn't look like work, sitting by the desk does
  • A more creative output is something that is hard to measure quantitative
  • Not going with an existing solution might be seen as indecisiveness
  • 20% boost of productivity (yep, that's a day a week)

Least amount of effort

  • Good start: reflecting on how and why things are done like they are
  • Start/stop/continue with
  • Don't rush for a solution
  • Be silly/stupid/funny/different
  • If you are managing people, don't tell them what they should do, ask them to solve a problem.
